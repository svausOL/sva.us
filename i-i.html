MICRO-LENDING                          
During my voluntary teaching period at An Giang University, I discovered that a majority of the students are from rural areas within the province, with quite a few of them coming from economically disadvantaged families. These students have endured years of hardship and faced numerous challenges to reach the university. Owing to cultural factors, agricultural labour demand, and economic difficulties within their families, numerous rural children are compelled to abandon their schooling prematurely. Nevertheless, a few exceptional students have exhibited remarkable resilience under immense pressure, relying solely on their unwavering determination to secure scholarships from both the school and the government, enabling them to pursuing education all the way to university. Despite the meagre amount of the scholarships, they still manage to save a portion of the funds to support their families. Their perseverance is genuinely inspiring and heart-warming. By establishing a credit union catering to the education sector can offer more sustainable and far-reaching help to those in need.

TEACHERS
CO-OP

Although An Giang University is affiliated with the National University of Ho Chi Minh City, which is directly under the Prime Minister's Office, newly recruited teachers, even those with a Ph.D., only receive a salary of about $200, which is far from sufficient for their living expenses. Therefore, most teachers must seek additional income outside of their formal teaching positions.

In Long Xuyen City, the capital of An Giang Province where the University is located, private language centers typically only pay their teachers 30% of the tuition fees collected. Several of my outstanding students graduated last year and have become teachers themselves. They same face the reality of having to work many extra hours in order to earn enough to support themselves and their families. Establishing an educational cooperative, where teachers receive up to 70% of tuition fees can actually be a win-win scenario for both the lecturers and the students.
