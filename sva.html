SUMMARY

Experienced senior manager with over three decades of expertise in entrepreneurship, FDI project implementation, factory construction, staff recruitment, business development, salesforce alignment, business operations, and successful implementation of ERP and VAS accounting systems across diverse industries. With a strong dedication to driving growth and optimizing operational efficiency, I have consistently delivered outstanding results throughout my career. My extensive knowledge and versatile skill set enable me to excel in various business environments and effectively navigate complex challenges.


Prior to assuming the role of Business Development Manager at Caterpillar V-TRAC in Vietnam, I accumulated 16 years of professional experience in the United States. During my time in Seattle, Washington, I had the privilege of establishing and managing a successful SI (System Integrator) and network business, where I also served as a Financial Controller. In 1997, I was recruited by a subsidiary of a prominent Chinese electronic conglomerate to oversee operations and assume the role of Sales Director at their subsidiary in Dallas, Texas. These diverse experiences have equipped me with a broad skill set and a deep understanding of both the Vietnamese and American business landscapes.

Since 2004, I have actively pursued and nurtured client relationships in Vietnam, securing notable clients such as China Merchants Group (招商局) and Aluminium Corporation of China (中鋁集團) for Caterpillar. These experiences have allowed me to establish a strong track record in client development and project management and allowed me to leverage my expertise and contribute to the growth and success of these esteemed companies.

Vietnam has strict policies against re-leasing or subleasing industrial zone properties, except under specific circumstances. In this case, the client had constructed two factory buildings through separate investment projects and had signed a leasing contract with the German automotive company I.G. Bauerhin GmbH (IGB). However, despite multiple attempts by different consultants, the client failed to obtain approval from the provincial authority for over six months, resulting in a significant delay beyond the contractually allowed date.
